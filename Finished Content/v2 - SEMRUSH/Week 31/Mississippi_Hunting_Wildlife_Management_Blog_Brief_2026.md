### **1. Mississippi Deer Hunting Seasons: Zones, Dates, and the Rules That Actually Matter (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "mississippi hunting seasons," "deer hunting season in mississippi," "deer season for mississippi," "deer season in ms," "deer rut in ms"

**Opening (60 words):**
- Deer hunting drives the Mississippi season calendar — and it's more complex than most states because MDWFP divides the state into four deer management units: Delta, North Central, Hills, and Southeast. Season dates, weapon restrictions, and bag limits vary by zone. Know yours before you book a trip.

**Season Dates by Zone (175 words):**
- Velvet archery: Sept 12–14 statewide (private land and opted-in public land; special permit required)
- Regular archery: Oct 1 – Nov 21 across all zones
- Youth season: Nov 8–21 (youth 15 and under with a licensed adult)
- Gun season with dogs: Nov 22 – Dec 1; resumes Dec 24 – Jan 21 (varies by zone — check MDWFP for zone-specific dates)
- Primitive weapon: Dec 2–15
- Private land extended seasons in the Southeast Zone: gun season without dogs and primitive weapon season continue through Feb 15 — giving private land hunters a significantly longer window
- Bag limits: 3 antlered bucks and 5 antlerless deer per season statewide
- Mandatory harvest reporting required; CWD sampling mandatory in designated areas — know if your county is on the list before you go

**The 7-Day Rule Explained (100 words):**
- Mississippi's "7-day rule" is one of the most-searched and most-misunderstood deer regulations in the state. Hunters frequently ask: does it still apply?

**Best Counties and Zones for Deer (65 words):**

### **5. Mississippi Hunting Licenses, Tags & Regulations: What You Actually Need (350 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "mississippi hunting seasons," "mississippi hunting laws on private land," "ms deer season 2026"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Getting your Mississippi hunting license right before season starts saves headaches at the check station. Here's what most hunters need and a few rules that trip people up every fall.

**License Breakdown (150 words):**
- Resident annual hunting license: base license required for all hunting; species-specific licenses stack on top
- Non-resident hunting license: significantly higher cost than resident; valid for deer, dove, small game (species-dependent)
- Archery license: required for archery deer season (separate from base license)
- Turkey tag (2026 change): required statewide beginning 2026 season — purchase when buying your license
- WMA license: required to hunt any Mississippi Wildlife Management Area; not required on private land
- Combination fishing/hunting licenses available for multi-sport visitors
- License purchases: online at mdwfp.com, at license agents statewide, or via the MDWFP app

**Rules People Miss (125 words):**
- Mandatory harvest reporting: all deer, turkey, and alligator harvests must be reported to MDWFP within 24 hours via phone or online portal — failure to report is a violation even if the animal was legally taken
- CWD (Chronic Wasting Disease) zones: several Mississippi counties require mandatory CWD sampling; know whether your hunt location triggers this before you quarter an animal
- Baiting regulations: check current MDWFP rules — baiting rules have changed in recent years and vary by land type and zone
- Private land hunting rules: many regulations that apply to public WMAs (shooting hours exceptions, dog-hunting restrictions) do not apply to private land — hunters on private land often have more flexibility than they realize

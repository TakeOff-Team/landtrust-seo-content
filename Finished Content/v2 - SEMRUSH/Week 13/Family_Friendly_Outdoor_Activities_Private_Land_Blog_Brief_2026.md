## Notes for Content Writer

### Tone & Voice:

- **Voice:** Fellow outdoor parent sharing what you discovered — not a corporate brand, not a listicle machine; someone who genuinely believes private land access transformed their family's outdoor time
- **POV:** Second person ("you") — speak directly to the parent who's planning the activity
- **Avoid:**
  - Making this sound like an advertisement for LandTrust — the value should come through the activities described, not the pitch
  - Overly "outdoor lifestyle" jargon that alienates urban families new to this world
  - Generic phrases like "create lasting memories" — be specific about *what* the memory is (the morning a 9-year-old caught a 4-pound bass, the moment a kid saw their first deer at 20 yards)

### Key Messaging:

- **Core:** Private land offers what public parks can't — but most families don't know it's accessible and affordable
- **LandTrust angle:** The platform removes the barrier between families and private land; you don't need to know a landowner, own land, or belong to a hunting club — you just need a LandTrust account
- **The emotional through-line:** Screens vs. streams — the search for outdoor activities that actually disconnect kids from devices and reconnect them to the physical world; private land experiences are uniquely suited to this because the environment is immersive in a way a paved adventure park simply isn't

### Writing Tips:

- **Be specific:** Name the animal, name the state, name the activity in concrete terms — "catching a 3-pound largemouth on a private pond in Georgia" beats "great fishing opportunities"
- **Use the child's perspective:** Frequently shift to what the *kid* experiences, not just what the parent gets — "what it feels like to be the only person on a private lake for a morning"
- **Farm section is a discovery section:** Most readers won't have thought of farm tours as part of the "family outdoor activities" category — write it as a pleasant surprise, not an obvious inclusion

### **1. West Virginia Deer Season: Dates, County Rules, and What You Actually Need to Know (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "west virginia hunting seasons," "deer hunting season in west virginia," "deer season in west virginia," "west virginia deer rifle season," "west virginia archery season," "wv muzzleloader season"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Whitetail deer is the centerpiece of WV hunting culture, and the WVDNR has built one of the most layered deer season frameworks in the East. The basic dates aren't enough — you need to know about county-specific antlerless restrictions, the four counties closed to firearms, and what split-season means in practice.

**Season Dates by Method (150 words):**
- Archery and crossbow: September 27 – December 31, 2025 (statewide) — the longest season, no county restrictions
- Antlerless (split season, select counties): October 23–26 / November 24–December 7 / December 11–14 / December 28–31
- Buck firearms: November 24 – December 7, 2025 — **closed in Logan, Mingo, Wyoming, and McDowell counties**
- Muzzleloader: December 15–21, 2025
- Youth hunt (split): October 18–19 and December 26–27
- Winter Mountaineer Heritage Hunt: January 8–11, 2026 (deer, bear, turkey combined)
- Bag limits: 1 antlered deer per day; see regulations for antlerless privileges by county and weapon type
- Electronic check-in required before resuming hunting after harvest

**The Four Closed Counties Explained (100 words):**
- Logan, Mingo, Wyoming, and McDowell counties remain closed to firearms deer hunting — a long-standing management decision tied to terrain, deer population data, and hunter density in southern WV's coalfield region
- Archery and crossbow seasons remain open statewide, including these counties
- Hunters planning trips to southern WV should confirm their specific county status on WVDNR's hunt area maps before booking
- Private land access in open counties immediately adjacent to closed counties often sees less pressure — hunters from closed-county zones don't stray far

**CWD and Carcass Rules (75 words):**
- West Virginia has confirmed CWD in several eastern counties; WVDNR maintains a CWD zone map
- Hunters harvesting deer in CWD zones may not transport whole carcasses or heads with spinal column attached out of the zone
- Boned-out meat, cleaned skulls, and taxidermy capes are exempt
- Know your zone before the hunt — transporting a non-compliant carcass is a violation regardless of where you process it

**Pro Tip (25 words):**
"Book private land in the counties bordering the four firearms-closed zones — those deer don't know the county line, and the hunting pressure drops sharply."

### **4. WV Hunting Licenses: What to Buy, What It Costs, and How to Register (350 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "west virginia hunting license," "west virginia hunting license price," "wv hunting laws," "check in deer wv," "wv dnr deer check in"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Getting licensed in West Virginia is straightforward, but it does require stacking the right licenses for the species you're hunting. Understanding what's included in your base license — and what requires an add-on — saves time and avoids compliance gaps.

**License Structure (150 words):**
- **Base hunting license:** Required for all hunters before purchasing species-specific licenses; resident and non-resident tiers
- **Resident vs. non-resident:** Non-residents pay significantly higher fees; non-residents cannot hunt without a valid license even on land they own in WV
- **Species add-ons:** Deer, bear, and turkey each require species-specific licenses or stamps in addition to the base license
- **Hunter education requirement:** All hunters born after January 1, 1975, must show proof of completing a hunter education course before purchasing a WV hunting license
- **License purchase:** Online through wvhunt.com or at licensed vendors statewide; licenses available year-round
- **Suggested table for content writer:** License Type | Resident Price | Non-Resident Price | Notes (hunter ed req, add-on required, etc.)
- **Lifetime licenses:** Available for WV residents; one-time fee covers all base hunting privileges for life

[QUOTE: Out-of-state hunter on their first WV trip — what surprised them about the licensing process and how the stacking structure works compared to their home state]

**Electronic Check-In (100 words):**
- WV requires hunters to electronically register deer, bear, and turkey harvests before resuming hunting or transporting the animal
- Check-in is completed via the WVDNR mobile app or online at wvdnr.gov — no physical check station required
- The "7-day rule" referenced in PAA searches: this refers to the tagging and possession timeline — once you check in a harvest, you must process or freeze the carcass within compliance window; confirm current WVDNR language for exact terms
- Failure to check in before moving the animal is a common violation — do it in the field immediately after harvest

### **5. West Virginia Fishing Seasons and Regulations: Trout, Bass, and Private Water Access (350 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "duck hunting in west virginia," "west virginia fishing," "wv elk hunting"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- West Virginia's rivers and streams are legendary in fly-fishing circles — the Cranberry, the Greenbrier, the South Branch of the Potomac. The state's fishing regulations are less complex than hunting, but non-residents and first-timers still need to know the basics before they wade in.

**License and Basic Rules (125 words):**
- **Resident vs. non-resident fishing license:** Required for all anglers 15 and older; annual or short-term (1-day, 5-day) licenses available for non-residents visiting briefly
- **Trout stamp:** Required to fish designated trout streams — sold separately from the base fishing license
- **Special regulation waters:** WV designates catch-and-release only stretches and special slot-limit waters for trophy trout management; these are marked on WVDNR's fishing regulation maps
- **Season dates:** Most waters open year-round for warm-water species (bass, bluegill, catfish, crappie); designated stocked trout streams typically open mid-April with a put-and-take model
- **Brook trout:** Native streams may have special regulations — confirm with WVDNR before targeting native brookies
- **WV elk hunting connection:** WV has a limited elk herd in the eastern coalfields (McDowell, Mingo area); the elk lottery is extremely limited for non-residents — check WVDNR's elk page for current tag availability

**Private Water Fishing (100 words):**

- Fishing on private water through a LandTrust booking provides legally clear access without ambiguity about property boundaries or landowner consent

**Season Dates by Region (125 words):**
- **Deep South (Alabama, Mississippi, Georgia, Florida):** Season opens mid-March — earliest in the country; warm weather, green fields, vocal toms; Alabama typically opens March 15, Georgia March 22
- **Mid-South (Tennessee, Arkansas, Kentucky, Virginia):** April openers; peak gobbling activity; Alabama and Georgia are finishing up as these states open
- **Midwest (Missouri, Iowa, Kansas, Wisconsin, Michigan):** Late April–early May; excellent public land access but private land dramatically reduces competition for call-shy late-season toms
- **Northeast (Pennsylvania, New York, New England):** Early–mid May; shorter seasons (4–6 weeks); cold-weather gobbling often produces aggressive responses
- **West (most states):** Spring turkey available OTC; less competition than eastern states; often combined with spring scouting for fall big game

**Why Spring is Also Peak Draw Season (100 words):**
- While you're turkey hunting in April, western draw deadlines are closing — many hunters treat spring turkey as their "meanwhile" hunt while waiting for fall draw results

### **3. Summer Prep Window — July through August: Draw Results, Archery Openers, and Scouting (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "elk season dates by state," "deer season dates by state," "hunting season calendar by state"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**

**Draw Results and the Pivot Decision (100 words):**
- Most western states post draw results June–July: Colorado typically posts mid-June; Wyoming posts June–July; Montana posts July
- Drawing changes everything — confirm your dates, book lodging, buy tags immediately (leftover tags from failed draws sometimes go back on sale)
- Not drawing doesn't end the plan — leftover and secondary draw tags go on sale after results; OTC licenses remain available in some units; private land access can fill the gap without waiting another year
- This is the moment hunters who planned the two-calendar system either cash in or pivot — both require knowing your results the day they post

**Archery Seasons Opening in Late August–September (125 words):**
- **Western archery elk:** Late August–late September is peak rut for elk; archery elk seasons open late August in Colorado, Montana, Idaho, Wyoming; this is the most physically demanding and rewarding window in North American hunting
- **Whitetail archery:** Opens as early as September 1 in Wisconsin; October 1 in most Midwest states; archery deer season is the longest segment of the year (2–4 months in most states)
- **Mule deer archery:** Similar timing to elk — late August through September in western states
- Archery seasons reward scouting done in July–August; hunters who walk ground in the summer shoot deer in the fall
- Private land archery access is especially valuable — pressure-free stands and cameras that haven't been bumped before October

**Scouting and Private Land Access (50 words):**

**Midwest: The Rut Window Nobody Can Afford to Miss (100 words):**
- Midwest firearms seasons cluster around peak rut: Wisconsin's famous 9-day gun season runs Nov 22–30; Iowa's December muzzleloader wraps up a long season
- The November 1–15 whitetail rut window in the Midwest is the most sought-after two weeks in deer hunting nationally — bucks move in daylight, abandon normal patterns, and can be called
- Midwest whitetail hunting is dominated by private land — trespass pressure on public ground is high during peak rut; LandTrust properties across Iowa, Kansas, Missouri, and Indiana give hunters quiet stands during the most productive window

**Waterfowl: Flyways and Federal Frameworks (75 words):**
- Duck seasons run 60 days within federal frameworks: Atlantic Flyway October–January; Mississippi Flyway November–January; Central Flyway October–January; Pacific Flyway October–January
- Canada goose early seasons open September 1 in most flyways
- All waterfowl hunters need: Federal Duck Stamp ($25) + state waterfowl stamp + HIP registration + steel shot
- Private land waterfowl access eliminates competition for popular public marsh spots during peak migration

  - Exhaustive state-by-state tables (oneoutdoors.org already owns that; link to their data or link to state agencies instead)

  - Generic filler ("As every hunter knows...", "Hunting is a beloved tradition...")

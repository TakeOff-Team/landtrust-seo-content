### **4. Hunting Idaho Turkeys: Tactics for Mountain Merriam's (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "turkey hunting idaho," "idaho turkey hunting," "idaho merriam's turkey"

**Content Structure:**

**How Merriam's Behave in Idaho Terrain (125 words):**
- Merriam's are the subspecies throughout Idaho — white-tipped tail fans, mountain-adapted behavior, nomadic movement patterns
- Key behavioral traits Idaho hunters need to know:
  - Merriam's move vertically with snow — in early spring (April 8–15), birds may still be in valley bottoms; by late April–May, they've pushed up into higher elevation parks and timber parks
  - Idaho birds are vocal but terrain-sensitive — ridges and creek drainages interrupt sound travel; a gobbler 400 yards away in a drainage may not hear your calls at all
  - Hens lead the breeding dynamic early in the season; isolating a gobbler from his hens requires patience, not volume
  - Roost trees: cottonwoods along river bottoms and ponderosa pines on slopes — find the roost and you find the setup

**Calling and Setup Strategies (125 words):**
- "Work the ridge top calling, then get within a couple hundred above them and call them up" (Rokslide forum, 2020) — this elevation-positioning tactic is the most commonly cited Idaho-specific approach; getting above birds and calling them uphill is more effective than calling across drainages
- Private land advantage: on a LandTrust property you can position exactly where you need to be — set up overlooking the creek bottom strut zone, not just the closest legal public land you can reach
- Calling pressure: Idaho turkeys on private land respond to softer, less frequent calling than pressured public land birds; 3-4 soft yelps every 15–20 minutes outperforms aggressive cutting sequences
- Decoys: full strut tom decoy plus a hen decoy works well in open agricultural edges; avoid strut decoys in tight timber where approach angles are limited

**Early Season vs. Late Season Adjustments (75 words):**
- April 8–20: Birds are beginning to ramp up; aggressive calling can get responses but hens still dominatestructure
- April 21–May 10: Peak breeding window — most responsive period, classic gobbler-calling scenarios
- May 11–25: Late season birds are educated; switch to subtle hen calls and patience; late season birds on private land that haven't been pressured remain huntable when public land birds have gone completely silent

**Pro Tip (50 words):**
"Idaho turkey hunting from the valley floor is usually a waste of time. Glass the slopes from your truck at first light, locate a gobbler on his travel route, then position above him. Calling a Merriam's downhill is a fool's errand."

### **1. Delaware Hunting Licenses: What You Need, What It Costs, and the Sunday Hunting Rules (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "delaware hunting license," "non-resident hunting license delaware," "sunday hunting delaware," "delaware hunting regulations"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Before any season opens, you need the right paperwork. Delaware's licensing structure is straightforward — but non-residents will notice the steep price difference, and every hunter should understand the state's Sunday hunting rules before heading out.

**License Requirements (150 words):**
- Anyone born after January 1, 1967 must complete a hunter education course before obtaining a Delaware hunting license
- Residents (ages 16–64): $39.50 general hunting license
- Non-residents (ages 16+): $199.50 — one of the higher non-resident fees in the Mid-Atlantic region
- Junior license (ages 13–15): $5.00 resident / $50.00 non-resident
- Residents 65 and older: license exempt, but must obtain a License Exempt Number from DNREC
- Conservation Access Pass required for motor vehicles at public Wildlife Management Areas: $32.50 (Delaware-registered vehicles) / $65.00 (out-of-state vehicles)
- Purchase licenses through DNREC's Digital ePermitting system
- Table recommended: License Type | Resident Fee | Non-Resident Fee | Notes

**Sunday Hunting (100 words):**
- Delaware allows Sunday hunting — but the rules matter
- Private land: Sunday hunting is allowed with explicit landowner permission; without it, you cannot legally hunt on Sunday
- Public land: Only on designated public lands where DNREC has specifically authorized it; check each WMA before assuming Sunday access
- Species-specific exceptions: Gray squirrel and cottontail rabbit carry no Sunday hunting allowed (even on private land); check current DNREC regs for any changes
- Practical takeaway: Private land access is the most reliable way to secure Sunday hunting flexibility in Delaware

**Non-Resident Value Case (75 words):**
- $199.50 is steep — but Delaware offers the longest deer season in the region (5 months), spring turkey, waterfowl on the Atlantic Flyway, and gray squirrel and pheasant seasons extending through February
- For a hunter who will make multiple trips or spend weeks in-state, the per-day cost math works out quickly
- Compare: neighboring states like Maryland charge significantly less but have shorter, more restricted seasons

### **2. Delaware Deer Hunting Season: Five Months, Multiple Windows, and the Rules That Matter (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "delaware deer hunting season," "delaware archery season," "delaware shotgun season," "delaware muzzleloader season," "delaware deer hunting regulations"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Delaware's deer season runs longer than almost any state in the eastern US. Archery and crossbow open September 1 and don't close until February 1 — a full five months. Layered on top are muzzleloader, general firearm, special antlerless, and handgun/straight-walled rifle windows that keep hunters in the field from September through late January.

**Season Dates Breakdown (175 words):**
- **Archery & Crossbow (Statewide):** September 1, 2026 – February 1, 2027
- **Youth & Non-Ambulatory Weekends:** September 26–27, 2026 and October 31 – November 1, 2026 — two dedicated weekends before general season pressure
- **Special Antlerless:** October 2–4, October 23–25, October 30, and December 12–20, 2026 — targeted doe harvest windows to manage herd ratios
- **Muzzleloader (Early):** October 9–18, 2026 — 10 days during pre-rut
- **General Firearm (November):** November 13–22, 2026 — peak rut; 10 days of shotgun, muzzleloader, and handgun pressure
- **General Firearm (January):** January 16–24, 2027 — late-season cold-weather hunting
- **Handgun & Straight-Walled Rifle:** January 2 and January 4–9, 2027 — specialty weapon season
- **Muzzleloader (Late):** January 25 – February 1, 2027

**Bag Limits and Key Rules (125 words):**
- Maximum 2 antlered deer per license year (July 1 – June 30)
- Maximum 4 antlerless deer per license year; additional antlerless tags available for $20.00 each
- All deer must be registered with DNREC following harvest
- Hunter orange required during any firearm season: minimum 400 square inches on head, chest, and back
- Baiting prohibited: Delaware law prohibits using corn, grain, or any other attractant for the purpose of hunting deer — this includes dumping corn on the ground (answers top PAA query)
- The "7-day buck rule" is a Maryland regulation, not Delaware — a common point of confusion for hunters near the state line

### **3. Delaware Turkey Hunting Season: Spring Dates, Public Land Segments, and What You Need to Know (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "delaware turkey season," "delaware turkey hunting season 2026," "delaware spring turkey hunting"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Delaware's spring turkey season is one of the most structured in the Mid-Atlantic. The state segments public land hunting into four distinct one-week periods, limiting pressure on WMAs. Private land hunters enjoy the full statewide window — a significant advantage if you can secure access.

**Season Dates (125 words):**
- **Youth & Non-Ambulatory Weekend:** April 3–4, 2027 — two days of exclusive hunting before general pressure begins
- **Spring Season Statewide (Private Land):** April 10 – May 8, 2027 — full 29-day window on private lands with landowner permission
- **Public Land Segments (hunters must select one):**
  - Segment A: April 10–16, 2027
  - Segment B: April 17–23, 2027
  - Segment C: April 24–30, 2027
  - Segment D: May 1–8, 2027
- Bag limit: 1 bearded turkey per season (male only); spring season only
- Shotgun and archery allowed; rifles prohibited for turkey
- All turkeys must be registered with DNREC following harvest

**Public vs. Private Land Tradeoff (100 words):**
- The segment system on public land is intentional: it distributes pressure and gives each hunter a week of quality hunting rather than a month of overcrowded competition
- Hunters who draw Segment A (first week of season) typically encounter the most vocal, unpressured birds
- Private land hunters have the entire April 10 – May 8 window — nearly four times the hunting time of any single public segment
- This is one of the clearest arguments for private land access in Delaware: turkey hunting quality is directly tied to how many other hunters are on your property

### **4. Delaware Waterfowl Hunting: Atlantic Flyway Access, Season Dates, and What Stamps You Need (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "delaware waterfowl hunting," "delaware goose hunting," "delaware dove hunting season," "delaware duck hunting"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Delaware sits at the heart of the Atlantic Flyway — one of four major North American migration corridors. Its coastal marshes, tidal wetlands, and agricultural fields funnel migrating ducks, geese, doves, and teal through each fall. For waterfowl hunters, Delaware punches well above its geographic weight.

**Season Dates and Bag Limits (175 words):**
- **Mourning Dove (Early):** September 1 – November 28, 2026 | 15/day, 45 in possession
- **Resident Canada Goose:** September 1–25, 2026 | 15/day, 45 in possession — early-season local geese before migratory birds arrive
- **Early Teal:** September 19–27, 2026 | 6/day, 18 in possession
- **Ducks, Sea Ducks, Coots, Mergansers:** November 1–9, November 26–30, December 17, 2026 – January 31, 2027 | 6 ducks/day, 18 in possession
- **Migratory Canada Goose:** December 20, 2026 – January 3, 2027 and January 17–31, 2027 | 2/day, 6 in possession
- **Snow Goose (Statewide):** October 18, 2026 – January 31, 2027 | 25/day, no possession limit
- **Snow Goose Conservation Order:** February 1–6 and February 8 – April 10, 2027 | No limits — extends the season well into spring
- **Tundra Swan:** November 8, 2026 – January 31, 2027 | 1 per season (permit required)
- **Youth Waterfowl Weekend:** October 25, 2026 and February 7, 2027

**Stamps and Registration Required (75 words):**
- Delaware Migratory Waterfowl Stamp: $15.00 (required for all waterfowl hunters 16+)
- Federal Duck Stamp: ~$25.00 (required for all waterfowl hunters 16+)
- Federal HIP Registration: Free but mandatory for all migratory bird hunters (dove, duck, woodcock, etc.)
- HIP registration can be completed online; Duck Stamp available through USFWS
- Total extra cost beyond hunting license: ~$40 before you pull a trigger

**Identity Verification (100 words):**
- All LandTrust guests complete identity verification through the platform before booking

- Why it matters: anonymous access is the source of most bad actor scenarios; when someone knows their identity is on record with the booking, their behavior changes

**Review History and Track Record (125 words):**
- LandTrust's two-way review system creates a public behavioral track record for every guest:

**Booking Confirmation as Documentation (100 words):**

### **3. Your Property Rules, Enforced (325 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "how to protect your property from hunting guests," "private land hunting booking protection"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- When you list on LandTrust, you set the rules for your property. These aren't suggestions — they're the terms every guest agrees to when they book. If a guest violates them, you have documentation proving they knew the rules.

**What Rules You Can Set (150 words):**
- **Weapon restrictions:** Rifle only, archery only, shotgun seasons, crossbow — you specify exactly what's permitted and when
- **Access hours:** No before-sunrise access, no post-sunset activity, specific window during hunting hours
- **Guest limits:** Limit to 1 hunter per booking, or allow groups of up to 4 — your choice
- **Vehicle access:** Passenger vehicles only, no ATVs, no OHVs, no driving off established paths
- **Tree stand permissions:** Landowner provides stands only, or guests may use portable hang-ons with no screw-in steps, or no stands at all
- **Property area restrictions:** Hunters limited to specific fields or timber sections; agricultural operations off-limits
- **Check-in requirements:** Notify the landowner on arrival, park at the designated location, use the marked trail
- All of these rules appear on your listing and are presented to guests during the booking confirmation

**Why Rules in a Platform Agreement Matter More Than a Verbal Agreement (125 words):**
- Traditional access scenarios: you tell the hunter your rules when they knock; they nod; they may or may not remember or follow them
- Traditional lease: a written lease covers major terms but rarely anticipates every behavioral scenario
- LandTrust platform: your rules are part of the booking contract — every guest has read and agreed to them as a condition of access; if they violate a rule, the booking record documents that they knew the expectation
- When a dispute arises (and occasionally they do), this documentation is the difference between a he-said-she-said conflict and a clear, documented agreement with an accountability mechanism
- Platform dispute resolution can reference booking terms directly — the guest cannot credibly claim ignorance of rules they agreed to at booking

### **4. What Happens If Something Goes Wrong (325 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "what happens if a hunter damages your property," "hunting property damage by guests," "landowner protection hunting access"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Verification and rules reduce bad outcomes significantly. But the realistic question is: what happens when something does go wrong despite the system working as intended? Here's the process — step by step.

**Step 1: Documentation First (75 words):**
- Before any booking begins, document your property's condition: photograph gates, fences, structures, vehicles — any feature that could be damaged or disputed
- The LandTrust platform keeps the booking record; supplement it with your own pre-booking documentation
- After a booking ends, do a property walk before the next booking begins — most property issues are discovered in this window

**Step 2: Property Damage Coverage ($10,000 Per Incident) (125 words):**
- LandTrust provides up to **$10,000 per incident** in property damage coverage for damage occurring during a confirmed booking
- Triggers: the damage must occur during an active, confirmed LandTrust booking on your listed property
- Process: document the damage with photos immediately; report through LandTrust; a claim review follows
- What's covered: fences, gates, structures, vehicles, equipment damaged during a booking
- What to keep in mind: pre-existing damage is not covered — your pre-booking documentation is what establishes baseline condition
- The $10K coverage doesn't require you to purchase a separate policy; it's part of LandTrust's platform protection

**Step 3: Review the Guest (75 words):**
- After a booking, leave a review of the guest — specifically noting any rule violations, property condition issues, or concerns
- Your review protects other landowners who receive future booking requests from the same hunter
- The review system only works if landowners participate — honest negative reviews are as important to the platform's accountability infrastructure as positive ones

**Step 4: Dispute Resolution (75 words):**
- LandTrust provides dispute resolution support for booking-related conflicts
- All booking communication and terms are documented in the platform — start there
- If the damage exceeds normal reporting, LandTrust support can assist in resolving the dispute with reference to the booking record
- The platform's documentation advantage: everything is written down, dated, and agreed to at time of booking

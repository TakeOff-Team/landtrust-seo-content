### **1. New Hampshire Hunting Licenses: Costs, Types, and What You Actually Need (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "nh hunting laws," "NH hunting license," "new hampshire wildlife management units," "nh hunting season dates"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Before you worry about season dates, you need the right license — and in NH, that means understanding which licenses stack on top of each other, what the WMU system means for your specific hunt, and what's changed under the 2025 rule updates.

**License Types and Costs (150 words):**
- General hunting license: required base license for all hunters 16+ (resident vs. non-resident pricing); hunters under 16 do not need a general license but need species-specific licenses
- Apprentice license: allows new hunters to hunt with an experienced licensed hunter without completing hunter education first — a good entry point
- Species-specific add-ons: turkey, bear, and moose permits require separate licenses/permits on top of the general license
- Table recommended: License Type | Resident Cost | Non-Resident Cost
- Hunter education: required before getting a first NH license; online course available but in-person field day still required
- Licenses valid January 1 – December 31 each year; buy before your first hunt day

**Wildlife Management Units Explained (125 words):**
- NH is divided into 13 WMUs (A through M) that drive date variations for nearly every species
- WMU A covers the northernmost part of the state (White Mountains/North Country) — generally lower deer density, different firearms season structure than southern zones
- WMUs L and M cover the coastal/seacoast region — different waterfowl zones apply
- Practical shortcut: know your WMU before you look up any date; the NHFGD WMU map is the essential first step
- Most hunters in southern NH hunt WMUs G–K; northern hunters in WMUs A–C; coastal in L–M

**What Changed for 2026-2027 (50 words):**
- Crossbow now legal during archery season in select WMUs for any archery license holder — major access change
- Air rifles now legal for hunting moose, deer, and bear with additional NHFGD criteria
- Youth pheasant season added (September) — new entry point for young hunters
- Bear registration change: first bear must now be registered before taking a second

### **2. Deer Hunting in New Hampshire: Archery, Muzzleloader, and Firearms Seasons (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "deer hunting season in new hampshire," "deer hunting season new hampshire," "deer season in nh," "nh deer hunting regulations," "nh deer hunting zones," "nh youth deer season"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- New Hampshire's whitetail herd tops 100,000 animals, and the state runs one of the longest combined deer seasons in the Northeast — archery starts September 15 and the last late-season opportunity closes December 15 in most WMUs. Understanding the three-season structure is essential for planning your hunt.

**Archery Season (100 words):**
- Statewide open: September 15 – December 15 (WMUs B–M, any deer)
- WMU A exception: archery closes December 8 — plan accordingly if hunting North Country
- Crossbow now legal during archery season in select WMUs for any archery license holder (2025 rule change)
- Longer archery window than most northeastern states — 13 weeks of opportunity before firearms opener
- Bag limits: based on available permits and licenses; confirm antlered/antlerless restrictions per WMU at NHFGD

**Muzzleloader Season (100 words):**
- Statewide opener: October 31; closes November 10 — runs 11 days immediately before firearms
- "Any deer" vs. "antlered only" windows vary significantly by WMU and day within the season
- WMU-specific any-deer windows: example — WMU L and M (any deer the full October 31–November 10 window); WMU A and B (antlered only after October 31)
- Strategy note: hunters who prefer muzzleloader and want antlerless opportunities should prioritize WMUs L and M or southern zones with more flexible any-deer windows

**Firearms Season (125 words):**
- Firearms opener: November 11 statewide (tentative for 2026)
- "Any deer" window: varies by WMU — ranges from a single day (WMU A, November 11 only) to a full 12-day any-deer period (WMUs L and M, November 11–22)
- Antlered-only period follows any-deer window in all WMUs; statewide close December 6
- Youth Deer Weekend: October 24–25, 2026 (statewide, any deer) — separate youth-only opportunity ahead of muzzleloader and firearms seasons
- Baiting: allowed October 21–November 18 (WMUs A–L) and September 15–December 15 (WMU M only)
- 7-day buck rule: hunters may only take one antlered deer per season — the "7-day rule" refers to the mandatory 7-day waiting period before taking a second deer in some contexts; confirm current NH-specific interpretation with NHFGD

**Pro Tip (50 words):**
"The firearms any-deer window is the most competitive time on public WMAs. If you want a realistic shot at an antlerless deer, hunt the archery season early or target WMUs L and M where the any-deer window is most generous. Better yet, find private land with fewer hunters competing for the same deer."

### **3. Big Game Beyond Deer: Bear, Moose, and Turkey Seasons (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "bear hunting new hampshire," "moose hunting nh," "new hampshire turkey season," "new hampshire turkey hunting," "new hampshire turkey hunting season," "turkey hunting season nh"

**Opening (40 words):**
- NH's big game options extend well beyond whitetail. Black bears, wild turkeys, and the state's unique moose lottery round out the big game calendar and offer serious hunters additional reasons to plan multiple NH trips each year.

**Black Bear (100 words):**
- General season: September 1 through November 12 (WMUs A, B, C2, H1, H2, I2, K) or November 30 (WMUs C1, D2, E, F, G, I1, J1, J2, L, M)
- Baiting season: September 1–28 (most WMUs) — note: chocolate not allowed except white chocolate
- Dog season: September 22 – November 11 in most WMUs (H2, K, L, M closed to dog season)
- Second tag: available, but hunters must now register their first bear before taking a second (2025 rule change)
- NH bear population is healthy and growing; southern WMUs offer better access density
- Bear permits separate from general hunting license; confirm current permit requirements with NHFGD

**Moose Lottery (125 words):**
- NH is one of a handful of eastern states with an active moose hunt — a genuine bucket-list opportunity for New England hunters
- Season: nine consecutive days starting the third Saturday in October (2026 dates: October 17–25)
- Lottery system: applications open mid-January and close the last Friday in May; permits issued for specific WMUs
- Permit areas concentrated in northern NH (WMUs A–D) where moose populations are highest
- Non-residents can enter the lottery; permits are limited and competitive
- What the lottery doesn't tell you: what to do if you draw a tag but can't secure land access — private landowner permission is often required for moose hunting in the north country

**Turkey (75 words):**
- Spring season 2026: May 1–31 (the primary turkey hunting season in NH)
- Youth Spring Weekend: April 25–26, 2026 — priority access for young hunters
- Fall shotgun turkey: limited to select WMUs (D2, H1, H2, I1, J2, K, L, M); October 13–19, 2026
- Fall archery turkey: concurrent with fall deer archery season (September 15–December 15 in most WMUs)
- NH turkey populations have expanded significantly in recent decades; most huntable in southern and central WMUs

## Notes for Content Writer

### Tone & Voice:
- **Voice:** Experienced NH hunting buddy who's done the regulation research so you don't have to — knowledgeable, direct, practical; no bureaucratic language
- **POV:** Second person ("you") throughout — "You'll need," "Your WMU determines," "Before you book your hunt"

  - Copying regulation language verbatim from NHFGD — restate in plain English with the "so what does this mean for your hunt?" framing

  - Downplaying the WMU complexity — honesty about the zone system builds trust and makes the private land alternative (where you don't have to worry as much about zone-specific restrictions) more compelling

### Key Messaging:
- **Core:** NH hunting is excellent — but you have to do the homework on WMUs, license stacking, and season dates. This article is that homework, done for you.

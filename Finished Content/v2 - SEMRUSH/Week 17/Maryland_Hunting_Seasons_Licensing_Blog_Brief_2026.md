### **1. Maryland Hunting Licenses: What You Need, What It Costs, and How to Buy (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regulations," "maryland hunting license," "md hunting regulations," "maryland dnr hunting seasons"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Every Maryland hunt starts with the right license stack. The base hunting license is only the beginning — depending on what you're hunting, you'll likely need stamps, permits, or additional authorizations. Getting this right before opening day means no scrambling, no violations.

**License Types and Costs (175 words):**
- **Resident hunting license:** $24.50 (base, required for all hunting)
- **Non-resident hunting license:** $130.00 (base)
- **Junior hunting license:** $10.50 resident / $32.50 non-resident (under 16)
- **Senior license:** $5.00 resident / $65.00 non-resident (65+)
- **Apprentice license:** $10.00 resident (for first-year hunters hunting under licensed supervision — no hunter education required)
- **Species-specific additions:** Deer stamp, turkey stamp, migratory bird stamp, HIP registration (federal, free but required for migratory birds), federal duck stamp (~$30, required for waterfowl)
- All licenses purchased through MD DNR Compass system online or at license agents statewide
- Hunter education certificate required for all first-time hunters (exceptions for law enforcement/military)
- Table format recommended: License Type | Resident | Non-Resident

**Hunter Education Note (75 words):**
- First-time hunters must complete a Maryland-approved hunter education course before purchasing a license
- Apprentice license option: hunt legally under supervision of a licensed adult without completing education first — designed for new hunters trying the sport before committing
- Course completion can be done online (knowledge portion) plus a field day at approved locations statewide

**Pro Tip (100 words):**
"Non-residents: the $130 base license covers hunting most small game, deer, and turkey when combined with the appropriate stamps — but waterfowl requires a federal duck stamp and HIP registration on top of that. Build your license stack before you leave home using the Maryland DNR Compass system. Nothing worse than arriving on a marsh at 5 a.m. and realizing you're missing a stamp."

### **2. Deer Seasons in Maryland: White-tails, Sika, and the Region Split You Need to Know (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "deer hunting season in maryland," "maryland sika deer hunting," "maryland muzzleloader season," "maryland archery season," "sunday deer hunting in maryland," "sika deer season maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland's deer season runs from early September through late January, giving hunters one of the longest combined seasons in the Mid-Atlantic. But the key is understanding which season — archery, muzzleloader, or firearms — you're hunting, and whether Region A or Region B rules apply to your property.

**White-tailed Deer Seasons (150 words):**
- **Archery (Region A + B):** Opens early September through mid-October; resumes late October through late November; additional windows in December and into January — one of the longest archery seasons on the East Coast
- **Muzzleloader:** Mid-October and again in late December through early January
- **Firearms:** Saturday after Thanksgiving through mid-December; late season resumption in January (Region B)
- **Primitive Hunt Days:** February — long bow, recurve, or flintlock/percussion muzzleloader only; antlerless focus
- **Bag limits:** 2 antlered deer all seasons combined (1 per weapon season); antlerless varies by region — Region A: up to 2 per weapon season; Region B: 15 archery, 10 muzzleloader, 10 firearms (notably more liberal)
- **Sunday hunting:** Allowed in most counties for deer; county-specific list maintained by DNR — check before heading out

**Sika Deer Spotlight (150 words):**
- Maryland's Eastern Shore holds one of the only free-ranging sika deer herds in the United States — a Japanese species introduced to Assateague Island in the 1920s that expanded onto the mainland
- Sika bucks can weigh up to 110 lbs and carry distinctive multi-point antlers; hind legs, rump patch, and spotted summer coat distinguish them from white-tails
- Season dates largely mirror white-tailed deer (archery opens September, firearms open late November); separate bag limit of 3 sika per season (no more than 1 antlered)
- Prime habitat: Dorchester County marshes, Wicomico County farm fields, and private tidal wetlands on the lower Eastern Shore — the vast majority of quality sika habitat is private
- Out-of-state hunters specifically target Maryland for sika — it's a legitimate bucket-list hunt

**Pro Tip (75 words):**
"Sika deer are crepuscular — most active at dawn and dusk near marsh edges and agricultural fields. They behave more like elk than white-tails in rut, bugling and gathering harems. If you've never heard a sika stag bugle at first light on the Eastern Shore, it's an experience worth the trip. Plan for tidal marsh terrain and pack accordingly."

### **3. Maryland Waterfowl Hunting: Ducks, Geese, and the Atlantic Flyway Advantage (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland waterfowl hunting," "duck hunting season maryland," "maryland goose season," "md duck hunting season," "maryland waterfowl season"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland sits at the center of the Atlantic Flyway — the major migratory corridor running from Canada's boreal forests to the Gulf Coast. For waterfowlers, this geography matters. Chesapeake Bay, the Eastern Shore marshes, and the major river systems funnel enormous concentrations of ducks and geese through the state each fall.

**Canada Goose Seasons (125 words):**
- **Special Early Resident Canada Geese:** Eastern Zone opens September 1–15; Western Zone September 1–25 — resident geese that haven't migrated yet, excellent early-season action
- **Atlantic Population Geese:** December through late January — strict bag limit (1/day); these are the northern-breeding, federally managed geese with a separate season and limit
- **AF Resident Population Canada Geese:** Late November through early March — the main season, bag limit 5/day
- **Light Geese (Snow/Blue/Ross's):** October through late January; extended Conservation Order season runs into April — no daily bag limit or possession limit during conservation order period
- Chesapeake Bay's Eastern Shore counties are among the most productive goose hunting ground on the Atlantic Flyway; field blinds and decoy spreads on agricultural fields are the signature setup

**Duck Seasons (125 words):**
- **Eastern Zone duck season:** October split + November–December core + December–January extension; daily bag limit 6 (with species-specific sub-limits on black ducks, redheads, canvasbacks, etc.)
- **Western Zone:** Slightly different split dates; check current Maryland DNR zone maps
- **September Teal:** 10-day early season in September, teal only, 6/day limit — overlooked opportunity before the crowds arrive
- Species mix: mallards, black ducks, wood ducks, teal, diving ducks on the bay (scaup, bufflehead, goldeneye, canvasback)
- Federal duck stamp (~$30) required in addition to state migratory bird license and HIP registration

**Pro Tip (100 words):**

But nearly all of that productive agricultural land is private. Permission-based access or a LandTrust booking puts you in front of the birds that the WMA hunters never reach. Don't spend the season competing for blind spots on public water when the fields are where the action is."

### **4. Turkey, Small Game, and Other Maryland Hunting Seasons (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland turkey season," "turkey hunting in maryland," "squirrel season maryland," "coyote hunting maryland," "maryland pheasant hunting"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Maryland's hunting calendar extends well beyond deer and ducks. Spring turkey, small game, and predators fill the gaps between big seasons — and for hunters who want to stay in the field year-round, Maryland provides solid options.

**Turkey Seasons (125 words):**
- **Spring General Season:** Mid-April through late May (typically April 18–May 23); bag limit 1 bearded per day, 2 bearded per season
- **Junior Turkey Hunt:** Saturday in mid-April; statewide plus Sunday in specific counties
- **Fall Turkey:** Early November (approximately Nov 1–9); bag limit 1 either sex (fall and winter combined)
- **Winter Turkey:** Brief late-January window (approximately Jan 22–24)
- Best turkey habitat: Western Maryland mountains (Garrett, Allegany counties) and the far eastern counties on the Eastern Shore
- Turkey populations are strong statewide; spring season is the primary opportunity with consistent gobbling action
- Shotgun or archery only for turkey

**Small Game (100 words):**
- **Cottontail rabbit:** November through late February; 4/day, 8 in possession
- **Gray/red/eastern squirrel:** Opens early September (September 6) through late February; 6/day — one of the earliest small game seasons in the Mid-Atlantic
- **Ruffed grouse:** October through December; 2/day — western Maryland mountains only
- **Pheasant:** November through late February; 2/day either sex — limited habitat, primarily agricultural areas with brushy cover
- **Quail:** November through mid-January; private lands only in many counties; 6/day

**Predators (75 words):**
- **Coyote:** No closed season statewide; no bag limit — year-round hunting opportunity with no license required beyond the base hunting license
- Coyote populations expanding in Maryland; both Western and Eastern Shore hunters pursue them
- Electronic calls permitted; night hunting regulations vary — check current rules

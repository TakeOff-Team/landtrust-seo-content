### **1. Maryland Hunting Licenses: Types, Costs, and What You Actually Need (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regulations," "md hunting regulations," "maryland deer hunting regulations," "hunting calendar maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Before you worry about season dates, you need the right license in your pocket. Maryland's licensing system is tiered — resident, non-resident, junior, senior, apprentice — and several hunts require species-specific stamps on top of your base license. Here's what to buy and what it costs.

**License Types and Costs (175 words):**
- Resident hunting license (base license required for all hunting)
- Non-resident hunting license (significantly higher than resident cost — confirm current fees at compass.dnr.maryland.gov)
- Junior hunting license (available for youth; reduced cost)
- Senior lifetime license (one-time fee for qualifying residents)
- Apprentice hunting license (for new hunters hunting with a licensed mentor before completing safety education)
- Stamps required: Deer stamp (required for all deer hunters), turkey stamp (required for turkey hunters), migratory bird stamp (federal duck stamp + Maryland harvest information program registration for waterfowl)
- All licenses purchased through MD Compass online portal or licensed vendors statewide
- Hunter education certification required for first-time hunters or anyone without a license issued before July 1, 1977
- Table format recommended: License Type | Resident Cost | Non-Resident Cost | Stamp Required?

**Region A vs. Region B — Why It Matters (125 words):**
- Maryland divides deer management into two regions: Region A (Western Maryland — Allegany, Garrett, Washington counties) and Region B (all other counties including Eastern Shore)
- Bag limits differ significantly by region: Region B allows up to 15 antlerless deer by archery, 10 by muzzleloader, 10 by firearms; Region A limits are much tighter
- Season dates for certain weapons and antlerless opportunities also vary between regions
- Before booking a hunt, confirm which region the property falls in — it determines your legal bag limit and which seasons apply

"Check the Region A/B map before you buy a deer stamp — not because the stamp changes, but because your season calendar and bag limit do. A hunt in Garrett County operates under completely different rules than the same species on the Eastern Shore."

### **2. Maryland Deer Seasons: Archery, Muzzleloader, Firearms, and Dates (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "deer hunting season in maryland," "deer season in maryland," "maryland archery season," "maryland muzzleloader season," "maryland deer hunting regulations"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland's deer season is one of the longest in the Mid-Atlantic region — archery opens in early September and the season runs in some form all the way through February. Knowing exactly which weapons are legal when, and in which region, is critical before you step into the field.

**Archery Season (100 words):**
- Archery season for white-tailed deer opens September 5 — making Maryland one of the earliest archery openers in the region
- Runs through multiple segments into late January; Sundays may be included in specific counties depending on the year
- Antlered and antlerless deer both legal during archery depending on weapon type and region
- Sika deer archery season runs concurrently on Delmarva — a rare double opportunity for Eastern Shore archery hunters

**Muzzleloader Season (100 words):**
- First muzzleloader segment: typically mid-October (3 days statewide)
- Second segment: late December into early January
- Region B adds an additional muzzleloader antlerless-only segment (October 20–25 range)
- Antlered and antlerless rules vary by segment — confirm which applies before heading out
- Muzzleloader hunters are subject to deer stamp requirements; inline muzzleloaders generally legal statewide

**Firearms Season (100 words):**
- General firearms season opens the Saturday after Thanksgiving and runs approximately two weeks (historically November 29 – December 13)
- A late firearms segment in January applies in Region B (and select Region A days)
- Junior deer hunt days precede general firearms season — youth hunters get priority weekend access statewide and in specific counties
- No Sunday hunting on firearms season opener in most counties — verify your county's Sunday status before planning

**Primitive Hunt Days (50 words):**
- Maryland offers late-season primitive hunt days in early February (typically February 2–4) — flintlock and other qualifying primitive arms only
- These hunts are statewide and provide a final opportunity for deer hunters with remaining tags

**Pro Tip (25 words):**

### **3. Sika Deer Hunting in Maryland: The Eastern Shore's Hidden Trophy (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland sika deer hunting," "maryland sika deer season," "sika deer season maryland," "guided sika deer hunt maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (60 words):**
- Maryland is one of only two places in the United States where hunters can pursue wild Sika deer — a small Asian species introduced to the Delmarva Peninsula in the 1910s. The population is concentrated in Dorchester County on Maryland's Eastern Shore. For hunters who've never heard of a Sika deer, this is a bucket-list hunt hiding in your backyard.

**What Makes Sika Deer Unique (100 words):**
- Sika deer are cervids related to elk — vocalizations sound more like elk bugling than deer grunting
- Bucks carry small antlers; both sexes carry distinctly spotted coats in summer
- Sika inhabit Maryland's marshes and dense Eastern Shore woodlands — hunted differently than white-tailed deer; calling is effective; behavior resembles elk more than whitetail
- Population has grown steadily on Delmarva; the hunt is managed to maintain sustainable numbers
- No draw required — Sika are available under the standard Maryland deer license with a deer stamp
- Bag limit: 3 Sika deer per season (no more than 1 antlered)

**Season Dates and Methods (125 words):**
- Archery: Same calendar as white-tailed deer — opens September 5, runs through multiple segments into January
- Muzzleloader: Antlered or antlerless mid-October, antlerless-only segment late October; second segment late December–early January
- Firearms: Opens with general deer season (Saturday after Thanksgiving), with a late season in January
- Sika deer and white-tailed deer seasons run concurrently on the Eastern Shore — hunters can pursue both species under the same deer license
- Sunday hunting applies on many Eastern Shore counties for Sika deer — confirm county rules

**Pro Tip (40 words):**
"Sika deer hunting requires waterproof gear and serious patience — they live in marsh edge habitat that is wet, cold, and unforgiving in November. Book private land with marsh access; public Sika hunting areas are heavily pressured."

### **4. Turkey, Small Game, and Upland Birds in Maryland (350 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland turkey season," "turkey hunting in maryland," "maryland pheasant hunting," "squirrel season maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Beyond deer and waterfowl, Maryland offers strong turkey, small game, and upland bird seasons that extend the hunting calendar well into spring and provide excellent entry points for new hunters and those building out a full-season approach.

**Turkey (125 words):**
- Spring turkey season is Maryland's second marquee hunt after deer — general spring season runs mid-April through late May
- Junior turkey hunt day precedes general season (typically a Saturday in mid-April); youth hunters get statewide access the following Sunday in participating counties
- Fall turkey season is brief — typically early November (about 9 days)
- Winter turkey season: late January (3-day segment)
- Bag limits: Spring general — 1 bearded turkey per day, 2 per season; Fall/Winter combined — 1 either-sex
- No draw required; turkey stamps required on top of base hunting license
- Eastern Shore turkey populations are strong; western Maryland mountain turkeys are available but terrain demands are higher

**Small Game (100 words):**
- Squirrel season: Opens September 6 — one of the earliest small game seasons in the region; runs through February
- Cottontail rabbit: November 1 – February 28; daily bag limit 4
- Ruffed grouse: October – December 31; limited season in western Maryland mountain habitat
- Quail: November – January 15; available on private lands only in some areas
- Coyote: Open year-round with no bag limit — no license required for coyote hunting in Maryland on private land
- Small game license (included in base hunting license in most cases) covers all small game species

### **5. Maryland Waterfowl Seasons: Ducks, Geese, and the Atlantic Flyway Advantage (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland waterfowl hunting," "duck hunting season maryland," "maryland waterfowl season," "maryland goose season," "maryland duck hunting season"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland sits at the heart of the Atlantic Flyway — the primary migratory corridor for ducks and geese moving from Canada to the Gulf Coast. The Chesapeake Bay and Eastern Shore marshes concentrate more waterfowl per acre than almost anywhere in the eastern United States. For duck and goose hunters, Maryland is a serious destination.

**Duck Seasons (125 words):**
- Maryland splits waterfowl seasons into Eastern Zone (Eastern Shore, Chesapeake Bay) and Western Zone (Western Maryland and most of Central Maryland)
- Eastern Zone duck season: typically three segments from October through January (October 11–18, November 15–28, December 16 – January 31 in 2025-2026)
- Western Zone duck season: similar structure with slightly different opening dates (October 3–18 opener, then November and December segments)
- Species-specific rules apply: Black duck limits are more restrictive than mallard; species identification is mandatory
- Teal season opens earlier — September 18–27 for blue-winged teal (separate from main duck season)
- Federal duck stamp + Maryland HIP registration required in addition to state hunting license and migratory bird stamp

**Goose Seasons (125 words):**
- Canada goose hunting is the signature Maryland waterfowl experience — the Chesapeake Bay hosts one of the largest Atlantic Population Canada goose concentrations in the country
- Early Resident Canada Geese season: Eastern Zone opens September 1–15; Western Zone September 1–25 (early season, before migrators arrive)
- AF Resident Population Canada Geese: November 22–28, then December through early March — the prime goose season
- Atlantic Population (migratory) geese: much more restricted season and bag limits (1 per day in recent years) — hunters must distinguish between populations
- Light geese (snow geese): aggressive bag limits (25/day) during regular season; conservation order extends season with no limits into spring

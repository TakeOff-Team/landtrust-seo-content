### **1. Maryland Hunting License: What You Need, What It Costs, and How to Get It (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regulations," "maryland hunting license," "md dnr hunting," "hunting calendar maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Before you can hunt anything in Maryland, you need the right combination of licenses and stamps — and the list changes depending on what species you're targeting and whether you're a resident or non-resident. Getting this wrong before opening day is the easiest avoidable mistake.

**Who Needs a License (75 words):**
- All hunters 16+ must hold a valid Maryland hunting license; hunters under 16 may hunt with a licensed adult under the junior license program
- First-time hunters of any age must complete an approved hunter education course before applying for a license — Maryland offers both in-person and online options through hunter-ed.com
- All licenses purchased through the Maryland DNR's COMPASS system (compass.dnr.maryland.gov) — online purchase recommended; also available at licensed vendors statewide

**License Types and Cost Breakdown (150 words):**
- Recommend a table format: License Type | Resident Cost | Non-Resident Cost | Notes
- Key license categories to include:
  - Annual hunting license (base for all hunting)
  - Antlerless deer permit (required per county zone for antlerless-only hunting)
  - Archery stamp (required for archery deer hunting)
  - Muzzleloader stamp (required for muzzleloader deer)
  - Migratory game bird stamp (required for ducks, geese, doves)
  - Federal Duck Stamp (required for waterfowl hunting — purchase at USPS or online)
  - Sika deer: covered under standard deer license — no separate permit required
  - Black bear permit: lottery-based, limited quota; check DNR for annual application window
- Non-residents pay substantially more than residents across all license tiers — worth acknowledging upfront for out-of-state Sika and waterfowl hunters

**Application Timing (75 words):**
- Maryland license year: January 1–December 31 (annual license)
- Licenses available for purchase throughout the year; no separate application process for most species
- Black bear lottery: typically draws in late summer for fall season — watch for DNR announcement in June–July
- Hunting education certification is required before first license purchase; complete it well before you plan to hunt

**Pro Tip (50 words):**
"If you're a non-resident planning a Sika deer hunt on Maryland's Eastern Shore, check whether the landowner holds any antlerless permits for their zone before you book. These are county-specific and can affect which deer you're legally able to take during your hunt."

### **2. Maryland Deer Seasons: White-Tail, Sika, Archery, Firearms, and Muzzleloader (425 words)**

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland offers one of the longest deer hunting windows in the Mid-Atlantic — archery season opens in early September and runs in segments all the way through late January. White-tail hunters have multiple weapon seasons to work with; Sika deer hunters have a narrow geographic window that's worth planning around.

**White-Tail Deer Season Dates (150 words):**
- Recommend a table or structured list by weapon type:
  - **Archery (Antlered & Antlerless):** September 5 – October 15; October 19 – November 28; December 15–19; January 4–31, 2026 (with region-specific Sunday dates)
  - **Muzzleloader:** October 16–18; December 20 – January 3, 2026 (region-specific extensions apply in Region B)
  - **Firearms:** November 29 – December 13 (primary firearms season); January 9–11, 2026 (late season)
  - **Primitive Hunt Days:** February 2–4, 2026 (statewide)
  - **Junior Deer Hunt Days:** November 16 (statewide) and November 17 (certain counties)
- Bag limits: 2 antlered white-tail all seasons combined; antlerless limits differ dramatically by region (Region A: 2 total; Region B: up to 15 archery, 10 each for muzzleloader and firearms)

**Sika Deer: Maryland's Unique Trophy (150 words):**
- Wild Sika deer exist in only a few locations in North America — Maryland's Eastern Shore (particularly Dorchester County) is the primary destination
- Sika season dates largely mirror white-tail: archery opens September 5; firearms open November 29; see Region B rules for muzzleloader and Sunday dates
- Bag limit: 3 Sika deer per season, no more than 1 antlered
- These are small Japanese deer — bucks typically weigh 90–130 lbs — and they inhabit dense marsh and wetland habitat very different from typical Midwest whitetail terrain
- Hunting Sika on private marshland gives hunters a significant edge: public WMAs receive heavy pressure during the firearms season

**Sunday Hunting Note (50 words):**
- Sunday deer hunting is allowed in Maryland but varies by county — some counties remain closed, others open only certain weapon types on Sundays

### **3. Maryland Waterfowl Hunting: Ducks, Geese, and the Atlantic Flyway Advantage (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland waterfowl hunting," "duck hunting season maryland," "maryland waterfowl season," "md duck hunting season," "maryland goose season," "goose hunting season maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland sits in the heart of the Atlantic Flyway — one of four major North American migration corridors. The Chesapeake Bay and Eastern Shore marshes funnel hundreds of thousands of ducks and geese through the state each fall. For waterfowl hunters, this makes Maryland one of the most productive states on the East Coast.

**Duck and Coot Season Dates (100 words):**
- Maryland splits waterfowl into Eastern Zone and Western Zone (split roughly by I-95 and the Chesapeake Bay watershed boundary)
- **Eastern Zone ducks:** October 11–18, November 15–28, December 16 – January 31
- **Western Zone ducks:** October 3–18, November 22–28, December 16 – January 31
- **September Teal:** September 18–27 (early special season; excellent early-season opportunity)
- Daily bag limit: 6 ducks per day (3x in possession); species-specific restrictions for black ducks, canvasback, redheads — check current federal frameworks annually
- Federal Duck Stamp required in addition to Maryland migratory bird stamp — purchase both before opening day

**Goose Season Dates (100 words):**
- **Special Early Canada Goose (Resident):** Eastern Zone September 1–15; Western Zone September 1–25 — high-volume shooting before migrators arrive
- **Atlantic Population Canada Geese:** December 19 – January 3, January 14–31 (strict limits: 1 per day)
- **AF Resident Population Canada Geese:** November 22–28, December 15 – March 7 (5 per day)
- **Light Geese:** October 1 – November 28, December 15 – January 31 (25/day); Light Goose Conservation Order February–April (no limits)
- Maryland is one of the best states in the country for light goose hunting during the conservation order

**Private Land Access for Waterfowl (75 words):**
- Public blind spots on Maryland WMAs fill up fast — many require reservation systems for popular timber blinds on the Eastern Shore
- Private marsh and field access gives hunters the ability to set decoys in unpressured locations the birds haven't been trained to avoid

**Turkey Season (125 words):**
- **Spring Junior Turkey Hunt:** April 11, 2026 (statewide) and April 12 (specific counties with Sunday allowance)

### **5. Maryland's Hunting Regions, Public vs. Private Access, and Where to Hunt (325 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regions," "hunting in maryland season," "hunting season in md," "maryland hunting regulations"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland divides its hunting landscape into two main regions — Region A and Region B — and the difference matters more than most hunters think. Bag limits, season dates, and Sunday hunting rules all shift between regions. Knowing which region you're hunting is not optional.

**Region A vs. Region B Explained (100 words):**
- **Region A:** Generally covers the western counties (Garrett, Allegany, Washington, Frederick, Carroll, Baltimore, Harford, Cecil) plus Carroll and Baltimore counties — classified by the DNR as the "Fall Season" area historically; typically lower bag limits for antlerless deer
- **Region B:** Covers the southern and Eastern Shore counties, including Dorchester, Wicomico, Worcester, Somerset, Anne Arundel, Calvert, Charles, St. Mary's counties — higher antlerless bag limits reflecting a larger deer population and agricultural landscape
- Maps available on DNR website; recommend writer include or link a region map visual
- Sunday hunting availability also varies by county and region — always cross-reference DNR's county-level Sunday hunting chart before planning

**Public Land Options (75 words):**
- Maryland's Wildlife Management Areas (WMAs) span over 200,000 acres statewide — Chesapeake WMA, Millington WMA, Doncaster Forest, Green Ridge State Forest in Western MD are among the most productive
- Public land drawbacks: opening weekend pressure is significant on popular WMAs, especially during firearms season; deer learn quickly once pressure begins
- WMA parking and access can be competitive on peak weekends — arrive early or target weekday hunting

**Private Land Access (100 words):**
- Private land hunting in Maryland isn't just about exclusivity — it's about options that simply don't exist on public ground: hunting Sunday in counties where public land Sundays are restricted, accessing Eastern Shore marshes for Sika or waterfowl, setting up on agricultural fields for Canada geese without competing for a blind reservation
- LandTrust hosts Maryland private land hunting properties across the state — deer, turkey, waterfowl, and small game access available on farms and marshes that aren't accessible any other way
- For hunters making the trip from out of state specifically for Sika deer or waterfowl, private access is essentially a requirement; good public Sika spots book up before most hunters even start researching

### **1. Maryland Hunting Licenses: What You Need and What It Costs (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regulations," "md dnr hunting seasons," "md hunting regulations," "maryland deer hunting regulations"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Before you set foot in the field, you need the right combination of license, stamps, and permits. Maryland's licensing system isn't complicated once you understand the layers — but buying the wrong thing or missing a stamp can put your whole season in jeopardy.

**Base License Requirements (125 words):**
- All hunters 16 and older must have a valid Maryland hunting license (resident or non-resident)
- Hunter safety certification required for first-time license buyers (online course available)
- Licenses purchased through Maryland DNR's Compass system (compass.dnr.maryland.gov) — online, phone, or in-person at license agents
- Resident vs. non-resident: non-resident licenses are significantly higher; non-residents from states with reciprocal agreements may qualify for resident rates in some circumstances
- Table format recommended: License Type | Resident Cost | Non-Resident Cost | Valid Season
- License year runs July 1 through June 30 — plan purchases accordingly for fall seasons

**Stamps and Permits (150 words):**
- **Deer stamp:** Required for any deer hunting (separate from base license); covers all legal deer seasons
- **Sika deer stamp:** Required in addition to standard deer stamp for Eastern Shore sika hunting
- **Antlerless deer permit:** Separate permit required for antlerless-only seasons in Region B; limited availability, applied for through DNR
- **Turkey stamp:** Required for spring and fall turkey seasons
- **Federal and state migratory bird stamps:** Both required for waterfowl hunting — state Migratory Game Bird Stamp plus Federal Duck Stamp (purchased at post offices or online via USFWS); HIP registration also required
- **Bear permit:** Required for black bear season (western Maryland only); allocated by lottery — application deadline typically July
- Summary table: "What You Need for a Full Maryland Deer Season" — base license + deer stamp + antlerless permit (if Region B) = complete deer package

**Pro Tip (50 words):**
"Buy your stamps when you buy your license — don't wait. The Maryland Compass system lets you bundle everything in one transaction, and it's easy to forget a stamp until the DNR checks it. Non-residents: build the full license cost into your trip budget before you book."

### **2. Maryland Deer Seasons: Whitetail, Sika, and Dates by Season Type (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "deer hunting season in maryland," "deer season in maryland," "maryland archery season," "maryland muzzleloader season," "maryland sika deer hunting," "maryland sika deer season"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland deer hunting isn't one season — it's a layered calendar that runs from early September through late January, with distinct archery, muzzleloader, and firearms windows. Knowing which season overlaps with your target weapon, your region, and your access situation is the key to a productive year.

**Whitetail Deer Season Dates (150 words):**
- **Archery (Antlered and Antlerless):** September 5 – October 15; October 19 – November 28; December 15–19; January 4–31, 2026 (Region B: January 12–31)
- **Muzzleloader:** October 16–18; December 20 – January 3 (antlered and antlerless statewide); Region B adds October 20–25 antlerless window
- **Firearms (General):** November 29 – December 13 (the primary two-week firearms season); January 9–11 (Region B late winter season)
- **Primitive Hunt Days:** February 2–4 (statewide)
- **Junior Deer Hunt Days:** November 16 (statewide) and November 17 (certain counties) — youth hunters under 16 with a licensed adult
- Region A vs. Region B drives significant differences in antlerless bag limits — Region B allows up to 15 antlerless by archery, 10 by each firearm type
- Sunday hunting: varies by county (see Section 5)

**Sika Deer: Maryland's Hidden Trophy (150 words):**
- Sika deer — a small Asian elk species — were introduced to Assateague Island in the 1920s and have established wild populations across the Eastern Shore, primarily Dorchester and Wicomico counties
- Season dates largely mirror whitetail: archery from September through January, muzzleloader in October and December–January, firearms November–December
- Sika require a separate sika deer stamp in addition to the standard deer stamp — confirm before hunting
- Unlike whitetail, sika are often hunted in tidal marshes and thick phragmites stands — waders, chest-high boots, and patience in wet terrain are essential
- Private land access is critical: the best sika habitat on the Eastern Shore is privately held, with very limited public land in the prime marsh zones

**Pro Tip (50 words):**
"Archery season opens September 5 — before the rut, before pressure builds, and before anyone else has pushed the deer. If you have access to good private land in Maryland, that early archery window is your best shot at a mature buck on undisturbed ground."

**Turkey (125 words):**
- **Spring (general):** April 18 – May 23, 2026 — the primary season; bearded birds only; 1 per day, 2 per season
- **Junior Turkey Hunt:** April 11 (statewide) and April 12 (select counties) — youth hunters get first access
- **Fall Turkey:** November 1–9 — either sex; 1 combined with winter season

**Small Game and Upland Birds (125 words):**

### **5. Maryland's Hunting Regions and Sunday Hunting Rules (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regions," "sunday deer hunting in maryland," "hunting calendar maryland," "md hunting calendar"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Two questions Maryland hunters search constantly: "What region am I in?" and "Can I hunt on Sunday?" Both affect your season planning more than most guides acknowledge. Maryland's wildlife management regions determine your bag limits and some season dates; Sunday hunting rules vary by county and land type in ways that catch hunters off guard.

**Maryland's Hunting Regions Explained (150 words):**
- Maryland uses two primary deer management regions — **Region A** (Western Maryland: Allegany, Garrett, Washington counties) and **Region B** (all other counties)
- Region B has dramatically more liberal antlerless deer limits: up to 15 antlerless by archery, 10 by muzzleloader, 10 by firearms — compared to Region A's more conservative allocations
- What this means in practice: hunters in the Eastern Shore counties (Dorchester, Talbot, Queen Anne's, etc.) are in Region B and can take far more antlerless deer across the season
- The Region A fall season in western Maryland (Allegany, Garrett, Washington) runs on slightly different firearms timing in some years — verify with DNR's season calendar before planning a specific opening day
- Waterfowl seasons also split along Eastern Zone (primarily Eastern Shore and Chesapeake tidal areas) vs. Western Zone (inland rivers, western shore counties) — dates differ for ducks and geese

**Sunday Hunting (150 words):**
- Maryland allows Sunday deer hunting in most counties on private land and some state-managed public areas — but not universally
- Key rule: Sunday hunting for deer is permitted statewide on private land, but hunters must be at least 300 yards from any church or school in session
- Some wildlife management areas (WMAs) allow Sunday hunting; check WMA-specific regulations before assuming access
- Counties with historically restricted Sunday hunting (some areas near the Pennsylvania border) have seen rule changes in recent years — always verify current season regulations through DNR rather than relying on past-year memory
- Sunday hunting does NOT apply to turkey during spring season on some public lands — again, verify
- Bottom line: if you're hunting private land, Sunday deer hunting is generally available in most of Maryland — but confirm the specific WMA or public land rules if that's where you're headed

"Sunday is often the best day to hunt private Maryland land — public areas close to hunters while private land stays open, reducing pressure across the board."

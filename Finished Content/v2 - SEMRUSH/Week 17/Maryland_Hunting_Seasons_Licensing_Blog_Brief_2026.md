### **1. Maryland Hunting License & Permits: What You Need Before Opening Day (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regulations," "maryland hunting license," "md dnr hunting seasons," "hunting calendar maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Before you can hunt anything in Maryland — even on your own property — you need the right license. Maryland's licensing system is managed through the MD Outdoors online portal (compass.dnr.maryland.gov), and it was recently updated. Here's what you actually need, broken down by hunter type.

**License Types & Costs (150 words):**
- Hunter education requirement: all first-time hunters (or those without a license issued before July 1, 1977) must complete a certified safety course before purchasing
- Resident vs. Non-Resident licenses: resident annual licenses are substantially cheaper; non-residents pay higher base rates for all license types
- Core license required first: a base hunting license is required for all species
- Stamps and add-ons: Deer Management Permit (for antlerless deer), Federal Duck Stamp + Maryland Migratory Game Bird Stamp (for waterfowl), furbearer permit for trapping
- Junior hunting licenses available at reduced cost
- Apprentice hunting license: allows new hunters (without full certification) to hunt with a licensed mentor — good entry point for first-timers
- Table format recommended: License/Stamp Type | Resident Cost | Non-Resident Cost | Required For

**Key Regulations to Know (100 words):**
- Checking requirements: deer and turkey must be reported within 24 hours through the MD Outdoors app or online
- Bag limit tracking is the hunter's responsibility — Maryland's region-based antlerless limits make this especially important
- CWD (Chronic Wasting Disease) carcass transport restrictions apply in western Maryland counties — know before you drag a deer across county lines
- Coyote: year-round season, no bag limit, no additional license required beyond base hunting license

### **2. Maryland Deer Seasons: White-Tail, Sika, and the Regional Rules That Govern Both (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "deer hunting season in maryland," "deer season in maryland," "maryland sika deer hunting," "maryland archery season," "maryland muzzleloader season," "maryland deer hunting regulations"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland deer hunting runs from early September through late January — nearly five months of opportunity across archery, muzzleloader, and firearms seasons. But the rules change significantly depending on which region of the state you're hunting and which days of the week you're in the field.

**Region A vs. Region B Explained (100 words):**
- Maryland divides the state into Region A (western counties: Allegany, Garrett, Washington, and parts of Frederick) and Region B (everything east of that, including the Eastern Shore)
- Region B allows significantly higher antlerless bag limits — up to 15 antlerless deer during archery season — reflecting the population pressure on the Eastern Shore
- Region A has more conservative limits; antlered bag limits are identical statewide at 2 per season combined
- Sunday hunting: legal in most Region B counties on specific lands; several western counties remain closed — always verify the current county list with MD DNR before hunting Sundays

**Season Dates at a Glance (100 words):**
- Archery (White-tail): Opens September 5, 2025 — runs in multiple segments through January 31, 2026
- Muzzleloader: October 16–18, 2025 (first segment) and December 20 – January 3, 2026
- Firearms (antlered): November 29 – December 13, 2025; additional days January 9–11, 2026
- Junior Deer Hunt: November 16 (statewide); participation open to licensed junior hunters
- Primitive Hunt Days: February 2–4, 2026 — select lands only; check MD DNR for eligible areas
- Sika deer season dates mirror white-tail in many respects — confirm current sika-specific dates from MD DNR as some segments differ

**Sika Deer: Maryland's Bucket-List Species (125 words):**
- Sika deer (Cervus nippon) are native to Asia and were introduced to Maryland's Eastern Shore in the early 1900s — today the Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge and surrounding private marshlands hold one of the only huntable sika populations in North America
- Sika are smaller than white-tailed deer but highly vocal and challenging to hunt; their bugling during rut makes for an experience unlike any other North American deer hunting
- Best access: the Dorchester County marshes and surrounding private farmland on the Eastern Shore — most productive sika hunting happens on private ground, not WMAs
- Out-of-state hunters specifically travel to Maryland for sika; private land access through LandTrust can be the difference between hunting the edges of crowded public marshes and hunting prime private ground

### **3. Waterfowl Hunting in Maryland: Ducks, Geese, and the Atlantic Flyway Advantage (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland waterfowl hunting," "duck hunting season maryland," "maryland waterfowl season," "maryland goose season," "md waterfowl season"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (60 words):**
- Maryland doesn't just sit on the Atlantic Flyway — it sits at the heart of it. The Chesapeake Bay and its tributaries form one of the most important waterfowl staging and wintering areas on the East Coast. Canada geese, mallards, black ducks, teal, brant, and a dozen other species funnel through the state every fall, making Maryland's Eastern Shore one of the most productive waterfowl destinations on the continent.

**Zone Structure & Season Dates (125 words):**
- Maryland splits waterfowl seasons into Eastern Zone and Western Zone — dates and splits differ between zones, so confirm your zone before planning
- September Teal: September 18–27, 2025 — brief early season opener; both zones
- Early Canada Geese: Eastern Zone September 1–15; Western Zone September 1–25 — resident population birds before full migration
- Duck, Coot, Merganser: Multiple segments in both zones running through January 31, 2026; peak window December–January
- Atlantic Population Canada Geese: Limited season December–January; bag limit restricted to 1/day
- Light Geese (Snow/Blue/Ross's): Season plus Conservation Order with no daily bag limit; runs into April — Maryland is a prime light goose destination
- Federal Duck Stamp + Maryland Migratory Game Bird Stamp required for all waterfowl hunters
- HIP (Harvest Information Program) registration required — complete this online before your first waterfowl hunt

**Why Private Land Matters for Waterfowl (100 words):**
- Public hunting areas on the Eastern Shore attract intense pressure; blinds are often claimed before shooting light on popular mornings
- Private land access through platforms like LandTrust puts hunters in front of birds without the crowd — ponds, flooded fields, creek bottoms, and tidal marsh on private property often outperform public WMA blinds during the peak season
- Landowners with flooded cornfields or managed impoundments command premium demand; if you're not connected to private ground, you're hunting the birds that everyone else pushed off the public areas

**Pro Tip (50 words):**
"Maryland's Canada goose hunting is world-class on the Eastern Shore — but resident population birds (September season) and Atlantic Population birds (December–January) have very different bag limits. Don't carry an AP tag into a resident population hunt. Know which birds are in the air based on migration timing and season dates."

### **4. Turkey, Small Game & Black Bear: Maryland's Other Seasons (325 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland turkey season," "turkey hunting in maryland," "squirrel season maryland," "maryland dove hunting season"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Deer and waterfowl get the headlines, but Maryland offers a full slate of additional hunting seasons that keep hunters in the field from September through late winter. Here's what else is worth putting on your calendar.

**Turkey (125 words):**
- Spring season is the primary turkey season: Junior Hunt April 11, 2026 (statewide) → General season April 18 – May 23, 2026
- Spring bag limit: 1 bearded per day, 2 bearded per season (general); 1 bearded per day (Junior Hunt)
- Fall turkey: November 1–9, 2026; Winter: January 22–24, 2026 — combined fall/winter bag limit is 1 either-sex
- Maryland's turkey population is strong across the western counties and Eastern Shore agricultural land; birds in agricultural areas use fields heavily in fall
- Private farmland on the Eastern Shore and in central Maryland counties offers excellent spring turkey hunting with far less pressure than public WMAs
- No draw required; turkey stamps included with appropriate base license

**Small Game (100 words):**
- Cottontail rabbit: November 1, 2025 – February 28, 2026; bag limit 4/day
- Squirrel (gray, red, eastern fox): September 6, 2025 – February 28, 2026; limit 6/day — Maryland's squirrel season is one of the earliest openers, great for hunting while scouting deer woods
- Quail: November 1, 2025 – January 15, 2026 on select private lands only — wild quail populations are limited; most quail hunting is on released-bird private properties
- Pheasant: November 1, 2025 – February 28, 2026; 2/day either-sex — Maryland stocks pheasants on select WMAs; private stocked-bird operations offer more consistent action
- Dove: September 1 – October 18 (first split) with additional splits through January; 15/day bag limit; requires Maryland Migratory Game Bird Stamp

**Black Bear (60 words):**
- Maryland's black bear season is managed through a limited permit system; Region A (western mountains) only
- Archery and firearm seasons in October; applications required — not an over-the-counter opportunity
- Bear populations in Garrett and Allegany counties are healthy; applications open through MD DNR in spring
- 1 bear per hunter per season; check current MD DNR permit availability for exact season structure

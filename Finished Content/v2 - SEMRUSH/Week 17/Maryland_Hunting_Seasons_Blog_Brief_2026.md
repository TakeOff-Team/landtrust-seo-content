### **1. Maryland Deer Seasons: White-Tail, Sika, and How to Navigate the Calendar (425 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "deer hunting season in maryland," "deer hunting in md," "maryland archery season," "maryland muzzleloader season," "maryland sika deer hunting," "maryland sika deer season," "deer season in maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland deer season is a multi-layered calendar spread across three weapon types, two management regions, and roughly five months of open hunting. Understanding how it's structured — before you buy your first stamp — saves confusion and keeps you legal.

**White-Tailed Deer Season Breakdown (150 words):**
- **Archery season:** Opens September 5, 2026 — one of the earliest archery openers in the Mid-Atlantic. Runs in segments through late January 2027 with Sunday dates county-dependent.
- **Muzzleloader:** Two primary segments — October 16–18 (fall) and a winter segment late December through early January. Primitive hunt days (long bows, recurve, flintlock) in early February for antlerless deer.
- **Firearms (general gun season):** Runs approximately November 29 – December 13, plus a late segment in early January. Two weeks of general gun season — shorter than neighboring states; competition for public WMA access is fierce.
- **Bag limits by region:** Region A (Western MD) — 2 antlered total, antlerless limits apply by weapon type. Region B (Eastern Shore and much of Central MD) — antlerless limits are substantially higher (archery 15, muzzleloader/firearms 10), which drives significant antlerless deer harvest.
- **Sunday hunting:** Legal for white-tailed deer in most counties under specific conditions; sika deer also include Sunday hunting. Writer should include the plain-English summary and note which counties have restrictions.

**Sika Deer: Maryland's Marquee Species (150 words):**
- Sika deer are not white-tailed deer — they're a smaller Asian elk introduced to Dorchester County on Maryland's Eastern Shore in the early 1900s. Today, the Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge area holds the largest free-ranging sika population outside of Asia.
- Season dates largely mirror white-tail (archery, muzzleloader, firearms segments run concurrently), but sika hunting is concentrated in the marsh counties: Dorchester, Somerset, Wicomico, Worcester.
- Bag limit: 3 sika per season, no more than 1 antlered — more generous than white-tail limits.
- The hunt experience is fundamentally different from white-tail: dense marsh vegetation, tidal flats, rattling antlers, and bugles. Private land access in the Eastern Shore marsh corridor is how most serious sika hunters get away from the crowds at public WMAs.

### **2. Waterfowl Hunting in Maryland: Ducks, Geese, and the Atlantic Flyway Advantage (400 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland waterfowl hunting," "duck hunting season maryland," "maryland goose season," "maryland waterfowl season," "md duck hunting season," "duck season in maryland"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Maryland sits squarely in the Atlantic Flyway — the migration corridor for millions of ducks and geese moving along the East Coast each fall. That geography makes it one of the best waterfowl hunting states in the country, and the Eastern Shore specifically is in a class of its own.

**Duck Seasons and Zones (125 words):**
- Maryland waterfowl season is divided into **Eastern Zone** and **Western Zone** with different open dates for ducks, geese, and other migratory birds.
- **September teal:** Early teal season opens late September — a warm-weather opener before the main migration hits.
- **Duck, coot, merganser:** Eastern Zone opens mid-October (split season); Western Zone opens early October. Both zones run through late January.
- **Daily bag limits:** 6 ducks per day; species-specific sub-limits apply (black duck restrictions are tighter; pintail limits may apply — always check current federal framework before season).
- **Youth, Veteran, and Military Hunt days:** Two special days offered — typically one in November and one in February — worth noting for readers with eligible family members.
- Key point for writer: the exact split dates vary slightly year to year based on federal waterfowl frameworks. Direct readers to confirm current season splits with the Maryland DNR before booking.

**Canada Geese and Light Geese (125 words):**
- Maryland is Canada goose country. The Atlantic Flyway population and the resident population produce hunting opportunities from September through March.
- **Early resident Canada goose season:** September 1–15 (Eastern Zone) / September 1–25 (Western Zone) — before most other seasons open; good for hunters who want to start early.
- **Regular Canada goose season:** Runs from late November through early March depending on population and zone; daily bag limit varies (1/day for Atlantic Population, 5/day for AF Resident Population — these are different flocks with different management).
- **Light geese (snow, Ross's, blue):** Open October through late January with regular regulations; **Conservation Order** extends hunting with electronic calls and unplugged guns through April 15 — arguably the most liberal waterfowl hunting in the continental US.
- The Chesapeake Bay and Eastern Shore farm fields are the geography that powers this opportunity. Private farm fields with field blinds are the preferred access model — public hunting pressure on these spots is significant.

**Pro Tip (50 words):**
"The light goose Conservation Order is one of the most overlooked hunting opportunities in the Mid-Atlantic. No bag limits, electronic calls legal, unplugged shotguns — it runs through April 15 and the birds are moving in massive numbers. If you haven't hunted late-season snows in Maryland, you're missing something genuinely unique."

**Turkey Seasons (150 words):**
- **Spring turkey season 2026:** Junior Turkey Hunt — Saturday, April 11 (statewide) with a Sunday option April 12 in specific counties; General season — April 18 through May 23.

- **Winter turkey season:** January 22–24. One of the few states with a winter turkey segment.
- **Bag limits:** Spring general — 1 bearded per day, 2 bearded per season. Fall/winter combined — 1 either sex (fall and winter combined limit).
- The strongest turkey populations are in the far western counties (Garrett, Allegany) and on the Eastern Shore. Hunting pressure on public WMAs in Garrett County can be significant during the spring general season opener.

### **4. Maryland Hunting Licenses and Regulations: What to Buy Before You Go (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "maryland hunting regulations," "maryland hunting license," "hunting in maryland season," "hunting season in md," "md hunting regulations," "deer season in md"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Getting licensed in Maryland is a multi-step process. There isn't one hunting license that covers everything — you'll need a base license, then stamps and permits for specific species or weapons. Here's exactly what to buy and in what order.

**License Tiers and Fees (150 words):**
- All Maryland hunters must hold a valid hunting license before they go afield. Purchase through the Maryland DNR's Compass system online or at licensed vendors.
- **Resident regular hunting license:** $24.50
- **Non-resident hunting license:** $130.00 — a significant jump; out-of-state hunters should factor this into trip planning
- **Resident junior (under 16):** $10.50 | **Non-resident junior:** $32.50
- **Resident senior (65+):** $5.00 | **Non-resident senior:** $65.00
- **Apprentice license (first-time hunters, no safety course required):** $10 — limited use; must hunt with a licensed adult
- **Additional stamps required:** Deer and turkey typically require species-specific stamps; waterfowl requires a Maryland migratory game bird stamp plus a federal Migratory Bird Hunting Stamp (Duck Stamp), plus HIP (Harvest Information Program) registration
- Writer should note: first-time hunters must complete a hunter education course before purchasing a standard license; Maryland offers both in-person and online options

**Hunter Education and Key Rules (100 words):**
- Hunter safety certification required for all first-time hunters (exceptions for law enforcement and military).
- The **7-day rule** for deer: Maryland's tag system allows tagging antlered deer within a specific timeframe; writer should explain the current tagging and registration requirements in plain English (hunters frequently search "7 day rule deer Maryland")
- Baiting: No deer baiting on public land; baiting deer is prohibited statewide with very limited exceptions — writer should address this clearly as it's a common PAA query
- **Sunday hunting:** Legal in most counties for white-tail and sika deer; county-specific restrictions exist — recommend the writer include a brief county-by-county note or direct readers to the DNR Sunday hunting table

### **5. Hunting Maryland by Region: Eastern Shore, Central, and Western Highlands (375 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "Maryland Hunting regions," "Maryland Hunting regions map," "hunting in maryland season," "deer hunting in md," "maryland pheasant hunting"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Maryland is a geographically diverse state crammed into a small footprint. The Eastern Shore marshes, the Central farmland belt, and the Appalachian highlands of Western Maryland offer fundamentally different hunt experiences — knowing which region fits your target species is the first planning step.

**Eastern Shore (115 words):**
- The flatlands and tidal marshes east of the Chesapeake Bay are Maryland's waterfowl and sika deer capital. Dorchester, Somerset, Wicomico, and Worcester counties are the core zones.
- **What to hunt:** Sika deer (the main draw for out-of-state hunters), Canada geese and ducks (some of the best field hunting on the Atlantic Flyway), dove on harvested grain fields, spring and fall turkey
- **Private land relevance:** Eastern Shore agricultural land — corn, soybean, and milo fields adjacent to marsh — is the premium goose and dove hunting real estate in the Mid-Atlantic. WMAs exist but get hammered. LandTrust properties in this zone are genuinely high-value.
- [QUOTE: LandTrust landowner on the Eastern Shore on what out-of-state hunters consistently say after booking their first field goose or sika hunt on private land]

**Central Maryland (75 words):**
- The agricultural corridor stretching from the DC suburbs north toward Pennsylvania — Anne Arundel, Carroll, Frederick, and Howard counties — is white-tailed deer country. High crop agriculture, suburban interface, and managed forest create strong deer densities.
- **What to hunt:** White-tailed deer (archery and firearms), dove, rabbit, squirrel, some pheasant on private land
- Note: Sunday hunting is more restricted in some Central MD counties; writer should verify current county list

**Western Maryland — Appalachian Highlands (115 words):**
- Garrett and Allegany counties in Western Maryland are a different world: black bear, ruffed grouse, wild turkey, and white-tailed deer in mountain habitat.
- **Black bear:** Maryland's bear season typically runs a single week in late October in Allegany County and parts of Garrett County. Archery and muzzleloader options exist alongside firearms. It's a short, competitive season.
- **Ruffed grouse:** October through December; Western MD is the only reliable grouse habitat in the state.
- **Turkey:** Best spring gobbler hunting in the state is here; large tracts of mountain timber hold birds that are significantly less pressured than Eastern Shore flats birds.
- Private land hunting in Garrett County mountain timber puts hunters away from crowds during the notoriously short bear season.

### **4. Choke Selection — The Most Important Variable Nobody Talks About (300 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "turkey choke," "best choke for turkey hunting," "patterning turkey gun"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (50 words):**
- Lead with the argument: your choke matters more than your gun. A $350 Mossberg with a $60 aftermarket choke will outperform a $1,200 Benelli running a factory modified choke at 40 yards.
- This is the section that helps LandTrust rank for "turkey choke" (1,900/mo, peaks 6,600 in April)

**Choke Constriction Basics (75 words):**
- Extra Full / XX-Full / Full Turkey: different brands, same concept — tighter constriction for denser patterns at turkey distances
- Constriction measured in thousandths of an inch; most turkey-specific chokes run .660–.680" for 12 gauge
- **TSS Warning:** TSS #7/9 shot requires a special choke — most lead turkey chokes can damage or blow out if used with TSS; look for chokes specifically rated for TSS or hard shot

**Top Aftermarket Choke Recommendations (100 words):**
- **Primos Jelly Head:** affordable ($40–$60), widely available, excellent patterns with lead and copper-plated loads
- **Carlson's Choke Tubes:** great value, gun-specific fits, widely recommended in forums
- **Patternsmaster Code Black Turkey:** premium choice for TSS loads specifically ($80–$120)
- **Indian Creek Black Diamond Strike:** competition-grade pattern density at premium price ($100–$150)
- Key point: always buy a choke for your specific gun brand (Remington pattern ≠ Mossberg pattern ≠ Browning Invector)

**The Patterning Session (75 words):**
- Shoot at 25, 35, and 40 yards on a turkey head/neck silhouette target
- Count pellet strikes in a 10-inch circle — aim for 100+ pellets with lead, 80+ with TSS at your max intended range
- Know your maximum ethical distance before season — not after
- LandTrust angle: "If you can visit your leased property pre-season, pattern your setup at the specific distances you'll be shooting on that land."

**Pro Tip (25 words):**

### **5. Ammunition — Shot Size, Payload, and the TSS Revolution (250 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "best turkey load," "4 or 6 shot for turkey," "best shell for turkey hunting"

**Content Structure:**

**Opening (40 words):**
- Address PAA directly: "Is a 4 or 6 shot better for turkey?" — and introduce the TSS complication that makes this question more nuanced in 2026

**Traditional Lead & Steel (75 words):**
- #4, #5, and #6 shot have been the turkey standards for decades
- #4 = larger pellets, more energy per pellet, better penetration at longer ranges (35–45 yards)
- #5 = the sweet spot for most hunters — enough pellets for pattern density, enough size for reliable kill
- #6 = works well at 25–35 yards, especially in a tight choke pattern
- Winchester Long Beard XR and Federal Heavyweight (non-TSS) represent the best of this category

**TSS: The Game-Changer (100 words):**
- Tungsten Super Shot has a density of 18 g/cc vs. lead's 11 g/cc — TSS #9 hits harder than lead #5
- This is why a 20-gauge or .410 loaded with TSS can legitimately compete with 12-gauge lead loads
- Federal Premium Heavyweight TSS is the market leader; expect to pay $8–$12 per shell (vs. $2–$4 for lead)
- Worth it? For hunters who want to shoot smaller gauges, hunt in dense cover at close range, or hunt with youth: yes

**Simple Recommendation Table Note (35 words):**
- **Visual recommendation:** Cheat-sheet infographic: "Match Your Shot to Your Setup" — gauge / load / max ethical range / choke type combination table for quick reference

**Pro Tip (25 words):**
"Never shoot TSS through a choke not rated for hard shot. The density difference can cause catastrophic choke failure."

### **2. Florida Deer Season: What You Need to Know (300–325 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "deer season fl," "florida hunting dates," "archery season florida," "florida hunting season dates"

**Content Structure:**

**Overview of deer season structure (100 words):**
- Florida deer season runs in multiple phases: archery/crossbow, muzzleloader, general firearms, and antlerless-specific periods
- The zone system (Section 1) determines when each phase opens in your area
- Typical structure across most zones: archery opens in October → muzzleloader period → general firearms late November → late season January
- Legal deer: buck limits, antler point restrictions (zones vary), and antlerless deer season periods; LandTrust landowners can advise on what's typically seen on their property

**The WMA vs. private land experience (125 words):**
- Florida's public Wildlife Management Areas are heavily hunted and highly pressured — particularly in Central Florida where private land is limited
- WMA hunting often requires permit applications with lottery selection — popular areas (Apalachicola, Osceola National Forest, Ocala National Forest) are highly competitive
- Private land access through LandTrust eliminates the lottery barrier and the pressure of shared public hunting
- Deer density on managed private land in North and Central Florida is generally higher than adjacent WMAs — food plots, mineral programs, and reduced hunting pressure compound over years
- What to look for in a Florida deer hunting property: hardwood hammocks, food plots, palmetto flatwoods edge, swamp/cypress proximity

**Unique Florida deer hunting considerations (75 words):**
- Florida deer (Odocoileus virginianus seminolus) are smaller-bodied than Northern whitetails — expectation management for out-of-state hunters
- Axis deer on private ranches are an introduced exotic not subject to season restrictions — available year-round on participating properties
- Stand and blind hunting in Florida terrain: ladder stands near food plots are most common; still-hunting in palmetto and cypress swamp is a Florida-specific skill

### **3. Wild Hog Hunting: Florida's Year-Round Opportunity (275–300 words)**

**Target Keywords:** "hog hunt florida," "florida hog hunting season," "boar hunting season florida"

**Content Structure:**

**The year-round opportunity (100 words):**
- Florida has no closed season for wild hogs — they may be hunted year-round on private land with a valid hunting license
- Wild hog populations are established across all 67 Florida counties — one of the highest hog densities of any state
- Year-round availability makes Florida hog hunting the #1 off-season option for hunters who want to pursue game in summer months when deer, turkey, and waterfowl seasons are closed across most of the US
- High hog populations mean willing landowners are actively motivated to allow hunting — hog damage to crops, pastures, and habitat is significant

**What Florida hog hunting looks like (100 words):**
- Hunting methods: spot and stalk (daytime), bait piles (legal on private land in Florida), dog hunting (traditional Florida method — catch dogs and bay dogs), and night hunting with thermal or night vision (legal on private land in Florida)
- Where to find hogs: agricultural areas (soybean, corn, pasture — hogs follow food), river bottoms and creek drainages, palmetto flatwoods and piney woods
- Species note: Florida wild hogs are a mix of European wild boar and feral domestic hog genetics — mature boars can exceed 300 lbs, though average is 100–150 lbs

**Private land advantage for hog hunting (75 words):**
- Hog hunting on WMAs in Florida is restricted by season rules designed for deer management
- Private land allows year-round access, night hunting, baiting, and dog hunting — all legal methods on private land that are restricted on public

- License requirement: valid Florida hunting license + alligator trapper license + harvest permit

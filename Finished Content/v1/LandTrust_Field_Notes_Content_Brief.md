## Article Structure & Outline
1) Hook/Intro (90–120 words)
- Problem: static e‑scouting misses on‑the‑ground changes.
- Solution: Field Notes = owner-sourced, real-time updates tied to listings.

2) What Field Notes Is (and Why It’s Different)
- Owner-authored updates vs generic map layers.
- Examples: fresh trail-cam pics, crop/food plot status, access notes, amenities, recent pressure.

3) Set Up in 60 Seconds (Step‑by‑Step)
- Find a listing → tap Follow → enable notifications → choose channels (email/push).
- Where Field Notes live on a listing page. How to filter view.

4) Read Updates Like a Pro (Turn Notes Into a Plan)
- Timing cues: new water, fresh sign, feed, pressure windows.
- Access/parking changes; wind setup hints; blind relocations.
- Quick decision tree: book now, wait for next update, message owner.

5) Pre‑Hunt Workflow Using Field Notes
- 7–3 days out: watch for habitat/pressure updates; confirm access windows.
- 48–24 hours: align weather/wind with updates; finalize stand/approach.
- Morning-of: re‑check updates; message owner if any change.

6) E‑Scouting + Field Notes: A Powerful Combo
- Layer Notes with maps (terrain, boundaries), recent imagery, and your pins.
- When to pivot properties based on new info.

7) Messaging Owners (Templates)
- Clarify rules, parking, quiet hours, shot direction; confirm recent activity seen in Notes.
- Short, respectful outreach that gets answers fast.

8) FAQs (AEO)
- How often are updates posted?
- Do I need Plus for Field Notes?
- Can I get push/email alerts?
- What content do owners typically post?
- Where do I see past updates?

9) CTA
- Follow 3–5 target listings today; turn on alerts; book with confidence.

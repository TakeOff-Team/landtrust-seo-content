### **2. EHD vs CWD: The Clear Differences**
- **EHD**
  - Cause & transmission: viral disease; spread by biting midges; outbreaks align with late summer–early fall; frost terminates vector activity. ([WI DNR EHD](https://dnr.wisconsin.gov/topic/wildlifehabitat/disease/Epizootichemorrhagicdisease))
  - Symptoms: fever, weakness, swelling of head/neck/tongue/eyelids; often found near water; survivors can show hoof ridges. ([Purdue Extension](https://www.purdue.edu/fnr/extension/be-on-the-watch-for-ehd-in-deer/))
  - Human risk: no evidence of human infection; do not consume visibly sick or found‑dead animals; cook venison thoroughly per state guidance.
- **CWD**
  - Cause & transmission: prion disease; spreads via animal contact and contaminated environments; persistent in soil and plants; universally fatal. ([CDC CWD](https://www.cdc.gov/chronic-wasting/about/index.html), [USGS FAQ](https://www.usgs.gov/faqs/what-chronic-wasting-disease))
  - Symptoms: progressive weight loss, drooping ears, lack of coordination/fear, excessive salivation/thirst. ([WI DNR CWD](https://dnr.wisconsin.gov/topic/wildlifehabitat/disease/Chronicwastingdisease))
- **Quick comparison (snippet target):** cause, spread, seasonality, environmental persistence, field handling, human risk guidance.

### **4. What Hunters Need to Do Now (Actionable)**
- **Pre‑trip checklist (snippet‑ready):**
  - Check latest state EHD/CWD pages, testing and carcass transport rules.
  - In EHD season: anticipate water‑adjacent scouting; report sick/dead deer promptly.
  - In/near CWD zones: know testing options; understand transport restrictions.
  - Book disease‑aware properties (Field Notes, local reporting transparency, clear rules).
- **In‑field best practices:**
  - EHD: avoid handling visibly sick deer; watch for clusters near water; time hunts around heat and movement windows.
  - CWD: minimize congregation (baiting); wear gloves; proper carcass disposal; testing workflow by state.
- **Meat safety and handling:**
  - EHD: not a human health risk; avoid visibly sick/dead animals; cook to safe temps per state guidance.
  - CWD: follow state testing; avoid meat from positive animals; dispose of carcass parts per regulation. ([CDC CWD](https://www.cdc.gov/chronic-wasting/animals/index.html))

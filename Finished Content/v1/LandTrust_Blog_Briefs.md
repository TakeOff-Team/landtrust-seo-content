## **Instructions for Writing SEO & GEO/AEO Optimized Blogs**

**To maximize your blog’s ranking and discoverability by both Google and AI/answer engines, follow these steps:**

1. **Use the Provided Structure:**
   - Follow the outline and section headers exactly as provided. These are based on real search demand and SERP features.

2. **Include Target Keywords Naturally:**
   - Use the primary keywords (from the brief) in your H1, H2s, and throughout the content, but keep the writing natural and human.
   - Sprinkle in related phrases and synonyms where appropriate.

3. **Answer Common Questions:**
   - Include clear, direct answers to “People Also Ask” questions and FAQs in the body text (preferably in their own sections or as bolded Q&A pairs).

4. **Use Data, Examples, and Lists:**
   - Support claims with real data, stats, or case studies.
   - Use bullet points, numbered lists, and tables for comparisons or step-by-step instructions.

5. **Optimize for Featured Snippets:**
   - Use concise, direct answers at the start of relevant sections.
   - Include summary tables, how-to steps, and definitions where possible.

6. **Geo-Targeting:**
   - Reference specific states, regions, or local programs where relevant.
   - Use phrases like “in [State]” or “near me” to capture local search intent.

7. **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization):**
   - Write in a clear, conversational tone.
   - Use short paragraphs and simple language.
   - Include Q&A, how-to, and list formats that AI/LLMs can easily extract.

8. **Internal & External Links:**
   - Link to LandTrust’s main site, relevant state resources, and authoritative sources.
   - Use descriptive anchor text (not just “click here”).

9. **Strong CTA:**
   - End with a clear call-to-action (CTA) that matches the brief (e.g., sign up, download, contact).

10. **Review for Human Tone:**
    - Make sure the writing sounds like a real person (Matt’s voice), not AI-generated or overly formal.

**If you follow these steps, your blogs will be optimized for both search engines and AI/LLM discovery.**
